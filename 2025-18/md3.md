45. Showbiz! - MIKE

> When MIKE doesn’t glide as a rapper, he is a sputtering engine: coughing, snorting, humph-ing and haw-ing, until somehow, as if summoned, the hardest shit ever comes spilling out of him. Long a beacon for the morbidly depressed, his gravelly baritone has shifted with age—not the croak of a lost teenager, but the lived-in voice of a legend who has survived to tell the tale. That grown-up commandeers Showbiz!, an album whose supernatural skill is offset by the humble human at its center. “I ain’t no prophet, nigga,” MIKE raps, and it’s a shame: He sure does sound like one. –Samuel Hyland

## 逐句翻译与解析

### 第1句
**原文**：When MIKE doesn’t glide as a rapper, he is a sputtering engine: coughing, snorting, humph-ing and haw-ing, until somehow, as if summoned, the hardest shit ever comes spilling out of him.
**翻译**：当MIKE作为说唱歌手没有流畅地说唱时，他就像一台噼啪作响的发动机：咳嗽、嗤鼻、哼哧着，直到不知何故，仿佛被召唤一般，最硬核的内容从他口中倾泻而出。
**解析**：
- **glide as a rapper**：作为说唱歌手流畅地表演。"glide"（滑翔）比喻说唱时的流畅、顺滑。
- **sputtering engine**：噼啪作响的发动机。"sputtering"（噼啪声）形容发动机运转不顺畅的状态，比喻MIKE在说唱前的酝酿过程。
- **coughing, snorting, humph-ing and haw-ing**：咳嗽、嗤鼻、哼哧着。这一系列动词生动地描绘了MIKE在进入最佳说唱状态前的准备和挣扎。
- **summoned**：召唤。这里用被动语态，暗示MIKE的说唱灵感仿佛被某种力量召唤而来。
- **hardest shit**：最硬核的内容。"shit"在这里是俚语，指说唱内容，"hardest"强调其质量高、冲击力强。
- **comes spilling out**：倾泻而出。形容说唱内容自然流畅地表达出来，与前面的"sputtering"形成对比。

### 第2句
**原文**：Long a beacon for the morbidly depressed, his gravelly baritone has shifted with age—not the croak of a lost teenager, but the lived-in voice of a legend who has survived to tell the tale.
**翻译**：长期以来，他一直是重度抑郁者的灯塔，随着年龄增长，他那沙哑的男中音发生了变化——不再是迷失少年的呱呱声，而是一位幸存下来讲述故事的传奇人物那饱经沧桑的声音。
**解析**：
- **Long a beacon for the morbidly depressed**：长期以来是重度抑郁者的灯塔。"beacon"（灯塔）比喻MIKE的音乐为抑郁者提供指引和希望。
- **morbidly depressed**：重度抑郁的。"morbidly"（病态地）强调抑郁的严重程度。
- **gravelly baritone**：沙哑的男中音。"gravelly"（碎石般的）形容声音沙哑、低沉。
- **croak of a lost teenager**：迷失少年的呱呱声。"croak"（呱呱叫）在这里形容少年时期不成熟、沙哑的声音。
- **lived-in voice**：饱经沧桑的声音。"lived-in"（住过的）比喻声音中蕴含着丰富的生活经验。
- **survived to tell the tale**：幸存下来讲述故事。这是一个常用短语，指经历困难后存活下来并分享经历。

### 第3句
**原文**：That grown-up commandeers Showbiz!, an album whose supernatural skill is offset by the humble human at its center.
**翻译**：这位成熟的艺术家掌控了《Showbiz!》专辑，这张专辑的超凡技艺被其核心处谦逊的人性所抵消。
**解析**：
- **That grown-up**：这位成熟的人。指前面提到的经历成长后的MIKE。
- **commandeers**：掌控、指挥。强调MIKE对专辑的主导和掌控力。
- **supernatural skill**：超凡技艺。"supernatural"（超自然的）强调MIKE的艺术技巧之高。
- **offset by**：被...抵消。这里指超凡技艺与谦逊人性的平衡。
- **humble human at its center**：核心处谦逊的人性。指MIKE在音乐中保持的真实和谦逊。

### 第4句
**原文**："I ain’t no prophet, nigga," MIKE raps, and it’s a shame: He sure does sound like one. –Samuel Hyland
**翻译**："我不是先知，兄弟，"MIKE说唱道，但这很遗憾：他听起来确实像一位先知。——塞缪尔·海兰德
**解析**：
- **I ain’t no prophet, nigga**：我不是先知，兄弟。"ain't no"是双重否定的口语表达，强调"不是"；"nigga"是黑人之间常用的亲昵称呼（需注意语境，避免滥用）。
- **it's a shame**：这很遗憾。表达作者对MIKE否认自己是先知的惋惜。
- **He sure does sound like one**：他听起来确实像一位。"sure does"是强调表达，突出MIKE的音乐具有先知般的洞察力和启示性。

23	Headlights Alex G

> On Headlights, Alex G surveys the iconography of his past work from the other side of 30 years old and a major label deal. In the process, he recovers the pieces of himself that had always loomed beneath the surface to construct his first true self-portrait. The lifelong Philly resident writes explicitly from his own perspective, putting the idealistic dreams of his youth and the character sketches of his old records in conversation with his present self: a new father and reluctant “career” musician. It’s a well-navigated turn toward greater vulnerability that comes alongside his prettiest songwriting yet. And, fortunately, his newfound maturity hasn’t stifled his eccentric impulses. “Bounce Boy” fully realizes his past experiments with Auto-Tune and hyperpop-adjacent electronica, while tracks on the record’s final stretch look to The Muppet Movie and Vince Guaraldi’s Peanuts scores to generate Headlights’ most tender moments. On “Is It Still You in There?” a children’s choir asks the titular question as jazzy keys float by. Despite the world-weariness and disillusionment that come with growing older, Alex G is still the same dreamer that he’s always been, with glimmers of optimism and awe beckoning around every corner of the record. –Jude Noel

> Dijon’s music has elastic relationship to time and place, surfing between eras, genres, and moods, creating something as kaleidoscopic as it is familiar. Throughout his second album, fragments of sounds — fiery ad-libs, golden age hip-hop samples, whizzing, inverted vocal riffs — jut out like beams of light piercing through the pitch black of night. Baby is indeed best described by that all-encompassing word, experimental, but Dijon can occupy a space closer to traditional R&B too, concluding “Kindalove” with a thematic summation: “When I need it you shock me with your love!” he belts, having just done the same to us. —J.I.
